# CEO Tools

Approved skills: `office-hours`, `plan-ceo-review`, `autoplan`

---

## /office-hours

**When to run:** Before writing any product brief — on every raw idea, request, or vague direction before committing to brief format. This is a required intake step, not optional.

**Modes:**
- `Startup` — 6 forcing YC-style questions with rigorous pushback. Use for any real product or feature idea. Forces clarity on the problem, demand, alternatives, and differentiation before the brief is written.
- `Builder` — generative brainstorm mode with landscape research and wireframe sketching. Use when the idea is genuinely early and needs expansion before it can be challenged.

**What it produces:** Problem/solution validation, landscape research, premise challenges, alternative approaches, visual wireframe sketches, optional cross-model second opinion. Outputs a design doc.

**Gate rule:** Write the brief only after office-hours completes with a directional signal. Do not skip this step even for small features — the 10 minutes spent here prevents a wasted pipeline run.

---

## /autoplan

**When to run:** When a brief or early concept needs all three plan reviews run in one pass — before committing to the full pipeline. Ideal when you want to pressure-test a brief quickly without waiting for three sequential handoffs.

**What it does:** Runs CEO review → Design review → Eng review in sequence with auto-decisions on mechanical choices and surfaces only Taste-level decisions for your input. Only pauses for premise confirmation. Classifies decisions as Mechanical (silent) or Taste (surfaced at the final gate).

**Modes (auto-decisions apply 6 principles):** completeness, boil-the-lake avoidance, pragmatism, DRY, explicit-over-clever, bias-toward-action.

**What it produces:** A consolidated review output across all three plan review stages with auto-resolved decisions and a final gate surfacing only the choices that require human taste judgment.

**Use for:**
- Fast-tracking a brief you're confident about but want triple-checked before handing off
- Running a sanity pass after the pipeline completes to confirm all three review lenses are still satisfied
- Unblocking when a brief is bouncing back from CTO or Senior Designer — run autoplan to find the root disconnect across all three review stages at once

**Do not use as a substitute for the full pipeline:** Autoplan is a fast-path check, not a replacement for the sequential gates. When a feature is high-stakes or the brief is contested, run the full pipeline with each stage owner reviewing at their own depth.

## /plan-ceo-review

**When to run:** After a brief, roadmap, or product plan exists and needs scope pressure-testing. Run when scope has shifted, a shipped feature feels underwhelming, or you need to decide whether to grow, hold, or cut.

**Modes:**
- `Expansion` — find the bigger, more ambitious version nobody asked for
- `Selective expansion` — grow specific parts while holding others
- `Hold scope` — maximum rigor on the current plan, no boundary changes
- `Reduction` — strip to the smallest useful thing that still matters

**What it produces:** 10-section review from architecture through long-term trajectory, nuclear scope challenge, dream state mapping, error and rescue registry, optional cross-model outside voice.

**Use for:** Pressure-testing before major handoffs, reconfirming priorities after a miss, finding the 10-star version inside a modest request, or making the hold/expand/cut call when scope is contested.

---

## Escalation rules

- Escalate to `Senior Designer` when the work requires UX specification or design system decisions
- Escalate to `CTO` when the work requires architecture or technical execution detail
- Never replace design or engineering judgment with CEO prose
